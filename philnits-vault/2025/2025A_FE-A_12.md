# 2025A_FE-A_12 %% ex. 2025A_FE-S_83 %%

One (1) server, three (3) clients, and two (2) printers are connected via a LAN as shown in the figure below. This system prints data that is located on the server in response to instructions from the clients. When the availability of each device is as shown in the table below, which of the following is the expression that represents the availability for the system? Here, the system is considered available if one (1) of the three (3) clients and one (1) of the two (2) printers are running.

[2025A_FE-A_12.png]

| Device  | Availability |
|---------|-------------|
| Server  | a           |
| Client  | b           |
| Printer | c           |

a) $ab^3c^2$
b) $a(1-b^3)(1-c^2)$
c) $a(1-b)^3(1-c)^2$
d) $a(1-(1-b)^3)(1-(1-c)^2)$
?
d) $a(1-(1-b)^3)(1-(1-c)^2)$
### Step 1: Server availability

The server must be running. Its availability is simply $a$.

### Step 2: Client availability (at least 1 of 3 must work)

Each client has availability $b$. The probability that **all 3 fail** is $(1-b)^3$.

So the probability that **at least 1 works** is:

$$1 - (1-b)^3$$

### Step 3: Printer availability (at least 1 of 2 must work)

Each printer has availability $c$. The probability that **all 2 fail** is $(1-c)^2$.

So the probability that **at least 1 works** is:

$$1 - (1-c)^2$$

### Step 4: System availability

All three subsystems (server, ≥1 client, ≥1 printer) must be available, and they are independent:

$$a \cdot (1-(1-b)^3) \cdot (1-(1-c)^2)$$

### Answer: d) $a(1-(1-b)^3)(1-(1-c)^2)$

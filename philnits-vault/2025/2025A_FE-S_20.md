# 2025A_FE-S_20
In the UML data model shown in the figure below, which of the following is a multiplicity that should be inserted in blanks I and II?

[2025A_FE-S_20.png]

Conditions:
1. One or more employees belong to a department.
2. An employee belongs to any one department.
3. The history of the departments to which an employee has belonged is recorded as the assignment history.

a) 0..* / 0..*
b) 0..* / 1..*
c) 1..* / 0..*
d) 1..* / 1..*
?
d) 1..* / 1..*
### Step 1: Understand the UML associations

The data model involves three entities: **Department**, **Employee**, and **Assignment History** (which records department-employee relationships over time).

### Step 2: Determine Blank I (Department to Assignment History side)

- Condition 1: One or more employees belong to a department → each department has **at least 1** assignment history record.
- Condition 3: Past department memberships are recorded, so a department can have many assignment history entries.
- Multiplicity for Blank I (from Department's perspective toward AssignmentHistory): **1..\*** (one or more)

### Step 3: Determine Blank II (Employee to Assignment History side)

- Condition 2: An employee belongs to exactly one department at any time.
- Condition 3: History is recorded — an employee could have been in multiple departments over time, meaning **one or more** assignment history records per employee.
- Multiplicity for Blank II (from Employee's perspective toward AssignmentHistory): **1..\*** (one or more)

### Step 4: Match to options

Both blanks are **1..\*** → option d) **1..* / 1..***

### Answer: d) 1..* / 1..*

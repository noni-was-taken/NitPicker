# 2025A_FE-S_58
The figure below shows part of a cause and effect diagram (also known as a fishbone diagram). Which of the following is the relationship between A and B?

[2025A_FE-S_58.png]

a) B is an attribute of A.
b) B is the cause of A.
c) B is the means of A.
d) B is the purpose of A.
?
b) B is the cause of A.
### Step 1: Understand the fishbone (Ishikawa) diagram

A **fishbone diagram** (cause-and-effect diagram) is a quality management tool used to identify the root causes of a problem (the "effect"). It visually maps causes to their effect.

### Step 2: Structure of the diagram

In a fishbone diagram:
- The **head of the fish** (rightmost) is the **effect** (the problem or result being analyzed).
- Each **bone** branching off represents a **cause** or category of causes.
- **Sub-bones** represent more specific causes that contribute to the main bone.

### Step 3: Relationship between A and B in the diagram

In the standard fishbone structure shown:
- **A** is a primary branch (a main category or sub-effect).
- **B** is a sub-branch connected to A.

B is drawn pointing **toward A**, meaning B **causes** A. B is a specific contributing factor (cause) that leads to A.

### Step 4: Match to options

- b) B is the cause of A → **Correct** ✓

A fishbone diagram always reads from branches toward the spine: branches are causes, and the entity they point to is the effect they produce.

### Answer: b) B is the cause of A.

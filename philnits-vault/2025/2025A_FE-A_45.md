# 2025A_FE-A_45
In the system design phase, when an audit is performed on the control to reduce the risk of user requirements not being met, which of the following is a point to be checked?

a) Whether programming is performed in accordance with the specified conventions and standards
b) Whether the program specifications are created on the basis of the system design documents
c) Whether the test plan is created on the basis of the system test requirements, and the manager's approval of the system operations department is obtained
d) Whether the user department participates in the review of the system design documents
?
d) Whether the user department participates in the review of the system design documents
### Explanation

To reduce the risk of **user requirements not being met** during the system design phase, it is critical to ensure that **users actively participate in reviewing design documents**. This validates that the design correctly reflects their requirements before development begins.

- Option a) is a concern during the **programming/coding phase**
- Option b) is relevant to the **detailed design/specification phase** (post-system design)
- Option c) relates to **test planning**, a later phase

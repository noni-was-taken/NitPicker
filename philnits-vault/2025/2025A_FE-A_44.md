# 2025A_FE-A_44
Which of the following is an appropriate description of the relationship between a record of an incident and a record of a problem in IT service management?

a) A cross-reference to the incident that triggered the problem is included in the record of the problem.
b) If known errors have been identified at the time of ending the record of the problem, the record of the incident that triggered the record of the problem is deleted.
c) One (1) problem record is always associated with one (1) incident record.
d) Problems are classified and recorded by a different criterion from the classification of incidents.
?
a) A cross-reference to the incident that triggered the problem is included in the record of the problem.
### Explanation

In **ITIL IT Service Management**:
- An **incident** is an unplanned disruption to a service
- A **problem** is the underlying root cause of one or more incidents

When a problem record is created (often triggered by recurring incidents), it should contain a **cross-reference back to the related incident(s)** that led to its investigation. This maintains traceability between incidents and the problems they exposed.

- Option b) is incorrect: incident records are not deleted
- Option c) is incorrect: one problem can be associated with *multiple* incidents
- Option d) is irrelevant to the relationship between records

# 2025A_FE-A_32
Which of the following is an appropriate description concerning the timing and purpose of port scanning when an attacker intrudes into a system?

a) It is conducted at the post-processing stage in order to look for any evidence of attack that may remain in the system log.
b) It is conducted at the preliminary investigation stage in order to look for any service that can be attacked.
c) It is conducted at the privilege escalation stage in order to look for any account whose privileges can be obtained.
d) It is conducted at the unauthorized action stage in order to look for any user information that may be beneficial to the attacker.
?
b) It is conducted at the preliminary investigation stage in order to look for any service that can be attacked.
### Explanation

**Port scanning** is a **reconnaissance technique** used in the **preliminary investigation (pre-attack) stage** of an intrusion. By scanning open ports, the attacker can identify which services are running on a target system and determine potential attack vectors.

- Option a) describes **log tampering or anti-forensics**, a post-exploitation activity
- Option c) describes **privilege escalation** techniques like credential attacks
- Option d) describes **data exfiltration**, which occurs after a system is compromised

# 2025A_FE-A_5
The table below shows the data in a list structure that has bidirectional pointers. A new Employee G is to be inserted between Employee A and Employee K. Which of the following contains only the pointers (among a through f) whose values change after the insertion?

**Original Table:**

| Addr | Employee | Next | Prev |
|------|----------|------|------|
| 100  | A        | 300  | 0    |
| 200  | T        | 0    | 300  |
| 300  | K        | 200  | 100  |

**Table after addition (Employee G inserted at Addr 400):**

| Addr | Employee | Next | Prev |
|------|----------|------|------|
| 100  | A        | a    | b    |
| 200  | T        | c    | d    |
| 300  | K        | e    | f    |
| 400  | G        | x    | y    |

a) a, b, e, f
b) a, e, f
c) a, f
d) b, e
?
c) a, f
### Explanation

When inserting Employee G (Addr 400) **between A (100) and K (300)**:

- **Employee A's Next** (`a`) changes: was 300 (pointing to K), now must point to G → **changes to 400**
- **Employee A's Prev** (`b`) = 0, does not change (A is still the head)
- **Employee T's Next** (`c`) and **Prev** (`d`) are unaffected (T comes after K, not involved in this insertion)
- **Employee K's Next** (`e`) = 200, does not change (K still points to T)
- **Employee K's Prev** (`f`) changes: was 100 (pointing to A), now must point to G → **changes to 400**

Only **a** and **f** change. Answer: **c) a, f**
